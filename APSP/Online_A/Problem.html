🧪 Problem: Tuition Path Problem!

You are a National Tuition Lord, constantly traveling between cities to tutor students. Since your time is valuable, every hour spent traveling costs you money. You earn a fixed amount of money per hour, so every hour spent traveling is a lost opportunity—we call this opportunity cost. But you are not alone. There are total K lords in the country, and you are one of them. You all have different hourly rates, thus different opportunity costs.

Each lord is located in a city (two or more lords can be in the same city). There is a weekly meeting of all lords in X hours. You need to find the meeting city. The meeting city should be the one where all lords can reach in time (i.e., each lord needs less than or equal to X hours to reach the meeting city) and their total opportunity cost is minimized. Here:

  • Opportunity Cost: The cost of lost opportunities while traveling. This is calculated as the product of hourly rate and hours spent in traveling.

⚠️ Special Note:

For this assignment, you are not allowed to use Single Source Shortest Path (SSSP) algorithms like Dijkstra or Bellman-Ford. Instead, you must implement a solution using All Pairs Shortest Path (APSP) approaches such as the Floyd-Warshall algorithm or equivalent logic. This ensures fair grading and emphasizes the intended learning outcome.

📥 Input Format:

The first line contains four space-separated integers: N (Number of Cities), M (Number of Roads), K (Number of Lords) and X (Hours remaining before meeting).

The second line contains K space-separated integers ai (current city of lord i).

The third line contains K space-separated integers hi (hourly rate of lord i and hi > 0).

The next M lines contain three space-separated integers: ui, vi, and ti. This means there is a road from city ui to city vi with travel time ti. The roads are bidirectional. 

N M K X
a1 a2 a3 ... aK
h1 h2 h3 ... hK
u1 v1 t1
u2 v2 t2
...
uM vM tM

📤 Output Format:

First print three space-separated integers: meeting city, total opportunity cost of all lords, and travel time of the last lord who reached the meeting city.

Then print K lines. ith line should contain the optimal path from ith lord's current city to meeting city (If current city and meeting city is same, just print the current city) and its associated opportunity cost.

If no meeting city is possible with given constraint, print "No meeting".

📦 Sample Input 1:

7 6 3 6
1 2 5
1000 500 2000
1 2 2
2 3 2
3 4 1
4 5 1
3 6 2
7 6 5

✅ Sample Output 1:

5 8000 6
1 -> 2 -> 3 -> 4 -> 5 6000
2 -> 3 -> 4 -> 5 2000
5 0

🧠 Explanation 1:

The meeting city is 5. The total opportunity cost of all lords is 8000. The travel time of the last lord (Lord 1) who reached the meeting city is 6.
