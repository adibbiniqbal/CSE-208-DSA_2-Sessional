The Shattered Kingdom

After the great war, the Kingdom of Eldoria lies in ruins. Cities are scattered, and many roads (called portals) are broken or corrupted. Some cities have been designated as capitals — the seeds of a new future — while others are now blocked and inaccessible due to destruction or danger.

You are tasked with establishing secure communication to several emergency posts across the kingdom. The portal network may contain negative-weight portals due to unpredictable magical influences, and negative cycles must be vigilantly detected: if reachable, they signify the emergence of an Abyss, rendering the entire mission impossible.

Problem

You are given:

  • A set of n cities numbered from 1 to n.
  • m directed portals between cities, each with an integer weight (possibly negative).
  • k capitals (starting points).
  • b blocked cities (completely inaccessible).
  • e emergency posts (target destinations).

Blocked cities are removed from the map: no portals can enter or leave them.

From any capital, you can start your journey. Your goal is:

  • For each emergency post, report the minimum cost to reach it from any capital.
  • If a negative-weight cycle is reachable from any capital, you must immediately declare “Abyss Detected”.
  • If an emergency post is unreachable, report INF.

Input

The first line contains five integers: n, m, k, b, e — the number of cities, portals, capitals, blocked cities, and emergency posts respectively.

The second line contains k integers: the indices of the capitals.

The third line contains b integers: the indices of the blocked cities.

The fourth line contains e integers: the indices of the emergency posts (in the order for which you must output answers).

The next m lines each contain three integers u, v, w — a directed portal from city u to city v with weight w.

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ n ≤ 10^5
  • 0 ≤ m ≤ 2 × 10^5
  • 1 ≤ k ≤ n
  • 0 ≤ b < n
  • 1 ≤ e ≤ n
  • -10^9 ≤ w ≤ 10^9
  • No self-loops.
  • No multiple portals between the same pair of cities.

Output

If a reachable negative-weight cycle exists, output:

Abyss Detected

and terminate.

Otherwise, output e lines, where the i-th line contains:

  • The minimum cost to reach the i-th emergency post,
  • Or INF if it is unreachable.

Notes

  • Blocked cities are completely removed from the network.
  • Capitals are valid starting points unless blocked (you may assume none are blocked at input).
  • Emergency posts may coincide with capitals.
